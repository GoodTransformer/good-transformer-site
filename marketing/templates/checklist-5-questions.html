5 questions to ask before
buying another AI tool

Most teams don’t need more tools — they need more confidence with the ones they have. Before the next licence, the next pilot, the next login, run through these five.

1

What decision or workflow will this actually change?

If you can’t name the work it improves, it’s another login — not a capability. Tie it to a real task before you tie it to a budget line.

2

Who owns it after the pilot?

A tool with no owner quietly becomes shelfware. Decide who runs it, who answers questions, and what “working” should look like in 90 days.

3

What happens to our data?

Know what it trains on, where data goes, and what must never be pasted in. Write the do-not-paste rule down in plain English, before anyone needs it.

4

Does it fit the tools we already have?

New tools should reduce sprawl, not add to it. Check it works with what the team already opens every day — or be honest that it replaces something.

5

How will we know it worked?

Agree one measure before you buy: time saved, quality lifted, or risk reduced. A measure, not a vibe — and a date to check it against.
