The 90-Day Small-Business
AI Adoption Planner

A smaller business does not need a three-year programme. It needs ninety controlled days that build evidence, capability and momentum. Work the three phases in order, and assign an owner to every line.

Days 1 to 30

Understand

Get an honest picture before you touch a tool. Almost every failed adoption skipped this.

Agree the leadership objectiveWhat is AI in service of? One sentence everything later is judged against.
Owner
Surface the AI already in useIncluding the quiet, unofficial kind. Without blame: it is your best evidence of what helps.
Owner
Run a light risk discoveryWhat data must never be pasted in, and where a confident wrong answer could do harm.
Owner
List candidate workflowsThe frequent, worthwhile, checkable tasks where AI is likely to help.
Owner
Take a baseline measureHow long the chosen work takes today, or how often it goes wrong. No baseline, no proof later.
Owner
By the end of phase one

A one-sentence objective, a map of current use, a written data rule, a shortlist of workflows, and a baseline you can measure against.

Days 31 to 60

Build

One or two pieces of work actually change. Two is a deliberate ceiling, not a starting point.

Select no more than two use casesThe best balance of value and low risk from your phase-one shortlist.
Owner
Create reusable instructions and contextThe standing prompt, house style, examples and definitions, so the work is owned by the business, not one person.
Owner
Test with a small groupInclude someone sceptical, not just the enthusiasts.
Owner
Record the failures honestlyThey are the most valuable output of the month: they show where human checks belong.
Owner
Establish human approvalWhich outputs a person must check, and who that person is, before anything goes live.
Owner
By the end of phase two

One or two workflows running on reusable, owned instructions, tested by real people, with the failure modes known and the human checks placed.

Days 61 to 90

Adopt

Decide whether this survives contact with normal working life.

Train the people who do the workUse your own real examples from the build phase, not a generic course.
Owner
Formalise the workflowWrite the short version of how it now runs, so it does not live only in one head.
Owner
Measure results against the baselineBe honest if the answer is disappointing. That honesty is the point.
Owner
Assign ownershipA named person responsible for keeping the workflow healthy as the tools change.
Owner

Then decide, for each use case

Stop

It did not earn its place. Say so plainly and free up the attention.

Improve

Promising but not there. Run another short cycle on the weak point.

Scale

It worked. Widen it deliberately, with the checks and ownership intact.
