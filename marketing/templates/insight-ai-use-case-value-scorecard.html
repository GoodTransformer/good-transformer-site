What each score means

Value — 1 hard to name a benefit; 5 a clear, statable prize (hours into fee-earning work, faster turnaround, errors avoided).

Activity — 1 happens rarely; 5 a high-frequency task, so the benefit compounds and the team gets many reps to learn.

Learnability — 1 “good” needs years of judgement to assess; 5 good output is knowable and quick to check.

Uncertainty — 1 complex, contested, errors are costly and hard to spot; 5 sits inside the technology’s strengths and a mistake is caught easily and cheaply.

Ease — 1 needs new systems, data access or sign-off; 5 runs on tools the team already opens, with little change.

Reading the total

Below 12

Not your first project. Park it and revisit once you have learned more.

12–18

Workable. Look hard at the lowest score before you commit, that is your risk.

19–25

A strong first candidate. Frequent, checkable, low-stakes and easy to stand up.

Warning flag

A candidate scoring 5 on Value but 1–2 on Uncertainty or Learnability is the seductive, brittle project, the one that impressed the room and will quietly teach your team that AI overpromises. High value alone is not enough. Favour the balanced score.
