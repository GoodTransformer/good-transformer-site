The AI Adoption Maturity Ladder

Adoption races ahead of impact because value has to climb four rungs, and a different thing blocks it at each. Tick the lowest rung where the answer is still uncomfortable. That is where you are.

The top: measurable, reinvested impact

Value shows up in the results, because the freed capacity was pointed at something that matters.

▲  climb in order  ▲
4

MEASUREMENT WITHOUT REINVESTMENT

You can see the saving, but it leaks: the freed time refills with more of the same work.

Next move: decide in advance where recovered capacity goes, and hold to it.

3

INTEGRATION WITHOUT MEASUREMENT

A workflow changed, but nobody measures whether it helped, so value stays anecdotal.

Next move: pick one honest outcome measure; take a baseline and track it.

2

COMPETENCE WITHOUT INTEGRATION

Skilled individuals, unchanged workflows, so the skill never compounds.

Next move: redesign one real workflow around the skill, not just train people.

1

ACCESS WITHOUT COMPETENCE

Everyone has the tools; few use them with judgement. Logins are not literacy.

Next move: build AI literacy and find a worthwhile first use case.

How to read it: start at rung 1 and work up. The first rung where the honest answer is “no, not really” is the one to fix, and fixing a higher rung first is wasted effort. Different teams can sit on different rungs.
