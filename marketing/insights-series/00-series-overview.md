# AI Lessons for Leaders: the 15-article series

This is the editorial workspace for the Good Transformer Insights series (15 articles,
each with a downloadable asset). The articles themselves are live under
`content/insights/`; this folder holds the series-level planning the public site does
not need. Companion files: `seo-metadata.md`, `source-logs.md`, `social-copy.md`.

---

## Editorial summary: the unifying argument

The series makes one argument from fifteen angles: **with AI, the value is in the
judgement and the organisational change, not in the tool or the access.** Adoption is
now easy and nearly free; results are not, because results depend on the unglamorous
work most organisations skip, choosing the right use case, redesigning the workflow,
measuring the outcome, keeping a human accountable, and reinvesting the time saved.

Every article attacks one specific place where a leader mistakes activity for progress:
naming tools for literacy, experiments for strategy, time saved for value, training for
adoption, output for judgement. The final article (the adoption-impact ladder) is the
synthesis: it shows the others as rungs of a single climb from access to reinvested
value. The voice is calm, evidence-led and sceptical in the right places, and the
commercial through-line is steady rather than loud: Good Transformer is the help for
doing the in-between work that turns AI from activity into result.

## Series name options

1. **AI Lessons for Leaders** (recommended). Matches the core offer exactly, so the
   series doubles as a content engine for it. Plain, searchable, on-brand.
2. **AI, with Judgement.** Captures the brand POV (judgement over hype) in three words.
   Better as a tagline than a search term; pair it with option 1 as a strapline.
3. **The In-Between Work.** Names the series' actual subject, the work between buying a
   tool and seeing a result. Distinctive and memorable, but needs the standfirst to
   explain it, so best for a hub hero rather than navigation.

Recommendation: use **AI Lessons for Leaders** as the series label and *AI, with
judgement* as the hub strapline.

## Recommended reading and publishing order

The articles were published in a deliberately varied order. For a reader arriving cold,
the most useful path runs in four movements, foundation to synthesis:

**1. Get your bearings (literacy and judgement)**
- AI literacy for leaders is not knowing every AI tool
- Assistant, automation or agent: what does your business actually need?
- What leaders should never delegate to AI

**2. Choose and value the work**
- How to choose your first serious AI use case
- AI productivity is not the same as business value
- Why intelligent people can become slower with AI

**3. Make it stick (strategy, people, governance)**
- Your team's AI experiments are not an AI strategy
- The AI skills gap starts at the top
- How to write an AI policy employees will actually use
- Every AI agent needs a job description
- Shadow AI is not just a security problem

**4. Embed and measure**
- AI training fails when the workflow stays the same
- AI can give one person the output of a team
- A practical 90-day AI plan for a small business
- AI adoption is rising. Why is business impact still uncertain? (capstone)

For an ongoing publishing cadence (one a week or fortnight), this order also works as a
release schedule: it opens with the most broadly useful pieces and closes on the
capstone that frames everything before it.

## Hub-page outline (/insights or a /insights/ai-lessons-for-leaders landing)

1. **Hero.** Series label ("AI Lessons for Leaders"), strapline ("AI, with judgement"),
   one-paragraph editorial summary (above, shortened), and the lead article card.
2. **The four movements.** Four sections matching the order above, each with a one-line
   description and the article cards in it. This turns fifteen posts into one navigable
   argument rather than a flat list.
3. **The tools.** A strip of the fifteen downloadable assets (scorecards, checklists,
   templates, planners), since these are the highest-converting part of the series.
4. **Where to start.** Three entry points by situation: "new to AI" (literacy),
   "tools but no results" (the ladder), "ready to act" (the 90-day plan).
5. **CTA.** The two routes: AI lessons for leaders (personal) and AI advisory for teams.

## Category and tag taxonomy

The drafts used a sprawl of tags. Recommended canonical set (six topic tags), with each
article mapped to one or two. Applying this set tidies the index tag filter.

| Tag | Meaning | Articles |
|---|---|---|
| `ai-literacy` | Understanding and judgement | literacy, when-ai-makes-you-slower |
| `strategy` | Direction, use cases, value | use-case, productivity-vs-value, experiments-vs-strategy, adoption-impact-gap |
| `adoption` | Making it real and lasting | 90-day-plan, why-ai-training-fails, one-person-team |
| `governance` | Risk, policy, accountability | what-not-to-delegate, ai-policy, agent-job-description |
| `leadership` | The leader's own role | ai-skills-gap-leadership, assistant-automation-or-ai-agent |
| `people` | Teams, culture, shadow use | shadow-ai, (cross-tag one-person-team) |

Keep `leadership` as a secondary tag on most pieces (the whole series is for leaders),
but lead each card with its primary topic tag so the filter stays meaningful. If the
existing posts use `ai-adoption`, fold it into `adoption` for consistency.
